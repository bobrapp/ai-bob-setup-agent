Executive summary

Bob runs a one-person AI agent agency. He sells AI employees — not "agent platforms" — to legacy SMBs that know they're falling behind but have nobody in-house who can stand up Hermes or Claude Code. The offer is unlimited agents, unlimited usage, unlimited support, priced at $5K/mo (OpenClaw tier) or $10K/mo (Hermes tier). The winning insight is that customers think they need ten agents and actually use one to three; pricing for the imagined need lets Bob deliver against the real need with healthy margin.

The bottleneck isn't sales. It's setup. Every onboarding is the same ritual — Orgo workspace, cloud computer per agent, Hermes install, MCP wiring, second-brain seeding, watchdog config, Telegram channel. ai-bob-setup-agent automates that ritual end-to-end so the practice scales beyond Bob's calendar. At 10 paying customers Bob is at $600K–$1.2M ARR with a self-managing fleet.

Problem

Roughly 99% of legacy businesses are behind on AI. They want an "AI employee" but can't assemble the stack. The capabilities exist — Hermes, OpenClaw, Claude Code, Codex — but the integration knowledge is rare. Most SMBs read about agents in newsletters and get stuck on step zero.

The current path to a working Hermes agent runs through a 15-minute call with Nick at Orgo, followed by manual setup. That doesn't scale for Nick, and it doesn't exist for most operators trying to build a services business in this space. The operator who automates the onboarding ritual gets the leverage.

Solution

ai-bob-setup-agent is a Python CLI plus configuration scaffold that performs the manual onboarding ritual end-to-end:

  1. Provisions an Orgo workspace dedicated to the customer.
  2. Spins up N cloud computers (one per agent).
  3. Installs Hermes or OpenClaw on each, with license and base image verified.
  4. Wires up MCPs — Perplexity, Context7, X — plus Composio and Agent Mail.
  5. Loads a customer-specific Obsidian-style second brain.
  6. Configures watchdogs with email and Telegram alerting.
  7. Hands Bob a Telegram channel through which a meta-agent manages the fleet.

Re-runs are idempotent. A new customer goes from signed contract to working agents in under 15 minutes of Bob's attention. Adding agent #2 or #3 to an existing customer is one command.

Customer

Two adjacent personas — Bob himself (the operator) and Bob's customers (the SMBs).

Operator: Bob

  • AI-fluent — has shipped Hermes / Claude Code projects before.
  • Comfortable in a terminal, Git, YAML.
  • Has 5–10 hours of weekly capacity blocked by the manual onboarding ritual today.
  • Either solo or one person + a contractor.
  • Owns the customer relationship; uses this tool internally.

End customer: the SMB

  • $2M–$50M annual revenue.
  • Owner-operator or CEO who feels the AI gap personally.
  • One operational pain that hurts now: BDR turnover, doc prep load, slow follow-up, etc.
  • Already pays $5K+/mo for at least one SaaS tool or freelancer team. Budget exists.
  • Doesn't want to learn what an MCP is. Wants an outcome.

Go-to-market

Content is overpowered for this audience and this budget. Nick's observation is that decision-makers in legacy verticals consume more LinkedIn and YouTube than they admit. The GTM motion is:

  1. Diverge. First 90 days: ship case studies in three verticals simultaneously (marketing, law, real estate). See where the market pulls.
  2. Converge. Sub-niche by the vertical that closes fastest. "Commercial real estate in Florida" beats "real estate." "Matrimonial law in New York" beats "law."
  3. Document loudly. Weekly Loom + LinkedIn thread of one specific automation, with a specific customer outcome (real name if they allow it, anonymized if not).
  4. Inbound from content. Customers who can hire Bob can also stalk LinkedIn. Two pieces of weekly content sustain a steady-state of 3–5 qualified inbound conversations.
  5. Outbound for the gaps. When inbound dips, run a two-week sprint of personalized cold outbound to a tight ICP. The bar is "would I take this call if I were them" — not volume.

Operations

The operating system is two layers: the setup agent (this repo) and the per-customer agent code that Bob writes for each engagement.

What ai-bob-setup-agent owns

  • Workspace provisioning + decommissioning.
  • Cloud computer lifecycle (create, image, configure, destroy).
  • Runtime install (Hermes / OpenClaw).
  • MCP and connector wiring.
  • Watchdog + alerting.
  • Telegram meta-agent integration.
  • Audit logging to docs/build-log.md.

What Bob still owns

  • Customer-specific agent prompts and tool selection.
  • Discovery calls and contract terms.
  • Second-brain seed content (a markdown file per customer).
  • The vertical-specific examples that show up in marketing.
  • Escalations from the watchdog beyond the auto-remediation set.

Risks

!
Platform risk on Orgo, Hermes.

Both are early. Pricing, capability, or availability could shift. Mitigation: keep the runtime abstraction in hermes_install.py thin enough to swap. Don't oversign multi-year contracts.

⚠
Model price collapse compresses the offer.

If model costs drop 10×, savvy customers ask why $10K/mo. Mitigation: the value is the operator + the setup + the watchdog, not the tokens. Re-frame upmarket if needed.

⚠
Big-platform competition (Salesforce, HubSpot bundling agents).

Likely. Mitigation: speed and specificity. A bundled enterprise feature can't sub-niche to "matrimonial law in New York" the way a solo operator can.

⚠
Single-operator burnout.

The whole thesis is that one person can do this. The watchdog and meta-agent exist precisely to keep Bob from being paged at 2 AM. If on-call hours creep above 5/week, treat it as a system bug and fix the automation.

Moat

Three durable advantages, in increasing strength:

  1. Speed of iteration. A solo operator with a strong setup agent ships customer-specific changes in hours, not sprints. Enterprise bundles can't compete on this axis.
  2. Sub-niche brand. Going from "AI agency" to "AI for matrimonial law in New York" creates a category of one. Defensible for years.
  3. Compounding operational data. Every customer makes ai-bob-setup-agent better at provisioning. Watchdog learns failure patterns. Second-brain seeds get sharper. Year-two Bob has tools year-one Bob's competitors don't.
